Overview

CURSR-IV is a high-altitude research rocket developed by the Chulalongkorn University High Altitude Research Club. My primary contribution was the avionics stack: a custom 4-layer SMT flight computer designed entirely in KiCad and manufactured via JLCPCB.

Hardware

  • 4-layer mixed-signal PCB (100 × 55 mm) with STM32F4 microcontroller
  • Onboard IMU (LSM6DSO), barometer (BMP388), GPS, and pyro channels
  • LoRa-based 915 MHz telemetry link (SX1276) with u.FL antenna connector
  • USB-C power delivery and dedicated LDO rails for analog and digital domains

Firmware

  • Bare-metal C on STM32 HAL; sensor fusion via complementary filter
  • State machine: idle → armed → boost → coast → apogee → recovery
  • Telemetry framing with CRC-16, ground-station decode in Python
